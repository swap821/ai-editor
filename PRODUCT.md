# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is the operator-builder of AI-OS: a technical owner working locally with a supervised, memory-driven coding agent, live backend, and 3D superbrain frontend. The context is repeated engineering work, not a marketing visit. The interface must support fast direction, visible system truth, and safe human authority.

## Product Purpose

AI-OS is a local-first, supervised cognitive operating system for building software with memory, tools, routing, verification, and approval gates. The superbrain frontend is the face of that system: the operator should see a living being that accepts directives, reacts to real backend events, and materializes work surfaces without faking capability.

## Brand Personality

Alive, exacting, sovereign. It should feel like a serious machine with a living body, not a dashboard theme or a generic AI assistant.

## Anti-references

Do not make it a flat HUD over a decorative brain. Do not add generic AI purple gradients, ornamental widgets, fake activity, decorative status dots, or dashboard chrome that is not part of the being. Do not weaken approval, audit, or local-first boundaries for visual effect.

## Design Principles

1. The body is the interface: every visible control is part of the being or grows from it.
2. Data must be true: visual activity maps to real backend, tool, memory, route, voice, or approval events.
3. The operator stays in authority: human approval, audit truth, and safety gates are visible and never bypassed.
4. Motion must explain state: arrival, attention, work, pause, and completion are cause-effect transitions, not decoration.
5. Preserve canon fidelity: the operator's brain asset, regional cortex, nervous-system anatomy, and browser sign-off own the final look.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for all DOM surfaces. Keyboard access, visible focus, touch targets of at least 44px, reduced-motion handling, and honest error states are required. Voice is a convenience channel only; typed input must remain available and spoken words must never authorize actions.
